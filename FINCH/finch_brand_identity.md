# Finch Brand Identity

## Brand Overview

**Brand Name:** Finch  
**Website:** applyfinch.com  
**Tagline:** Fewer Applications. More Interviews.  
**Hero Headline:** Turn Applications Into Interviews.  
**Core Positioning:** Finch is an intentional internship platform that helps students optimize for interview probability rather than mass-apply with low signal.

## Brand Summary

Finch is built for students who are tired of submitting large volumes of internship applications with little return. Instead of encouraging more applications, Finch helps users target stronger opportunities, tailor their materials, and improve visibility with decision-makers. The brand should feel like a smarter, more strategic alternative to the chaotic and discouraging internship search.

## Mission

To transform the internship application process from a frustrating numbers game into an intentional, data-driven strategy that helps students earn the opportunities they deserve while Finch streamlines, optimizes, and strengthens their path to interviews.

## Vision

To become the default infrastructure for intentional early-career recruiting by replacing cold, volume-based application systems with a smarter, outcome-driven approach where students are evaluated more efficiently and the path from education to experience is transparent and merit-based.

## Problem Statement

Students are often told to apply more and network more, but they are rarely given a system that actually improves outcomes. Many high-volume applicants submit 50 to 200 applications per cycle, get ghosted, receive little to no feedback, and waste large amounts of time on repetitive application work.

## Solution Statement

Finch replaces volume-based application behavior with a response-optimization system. It helps users:

- target high-signal roles
- tailor resumes and cover letters to specific jobs
- autofill applications across major ATS platforms
- reduce application time without sacrificing quality
- increase the likelihood of interviews

## Core Value Proposition

Finch helps students spend less time applying and improve their odds of getting interviews by turning application effort into a more intentional, strategic process.

## Target Audience

### Primary Audience

- Undergraduate students ages 19 to 23
- Engineering, Computer Science, or quantitative business majors
- Students actively applying for competitive internships
- Digitally native users who value speed, efficiency, and outcomes

### User Mindset

### 2. Speed without Quality Loss

### 4. User Control

### 5. Early-Career Advantage

Finch is designed for the realities of student recruiting and competitive internship pipelines.

## Product Narrative

### How Finch Works

1. Users sign up and connect LinkedIn.
2. Finch generates a rich candidate profile using AI analysis.
3. Users browse roles on ATS platforms like Greenhouse, Lever, and Workday.
4. Finch detects the application opportunity through the Chrome extension.
5. The platform generates a tailored resume and cover letter.
6. The extension autofills the application and stops at the final review page.
7. The user reviews and submits with confidence.

### Key Product Benefits

- less time spent on repetitive forms
- more tailored applications
- stronger ATS alignment
- improved recruiter visibility
- a more strategic internship search process

## Visual Design Direction: Morphism Strategy

Finch should use **conversion-focused SaaS minimalism** as the primary interface language, with **selective soft glassmorphism** used as an accent layer. The goal is to keep the product feeling modern, optimistic, and brand-forward without sacrificing clarity, hierarchy, or credibility. Finch should never look like a full glassmorphism experiment. It should look like a strong product UI first, with carefully placed translucent surfaces that add depth and polish.

### Recommended Approach

- Base the interface on clean SaaS minimalism with strong spacing, simple structure, and readable surfaces.
- Use subtle glassmorphism only where it adds emphasis, layering, or product polish.
- Keep any clay-like softness limited to small icon or illustration moments, not core layout containers.
- Do not use neumorphism, full claymorphism, or neobrutalism as primary visual systems.

### Why This Fits Finch

This approach matches Finch's brand personality: playful, innovative, and approachable, while still feeling credible and high-signal for students making career decisions. Finch needs to feel sharp enough for product trust, but warm enough to avoid looking cold, generic, or overly enterprise. Selective glassmorphism supports that balance by making key surfaces feel premium without turning the full experience glossy, noisy, or toy-like.

### Where to Use Glassmorphism

Use soft glass treatments intentionally on high-attention surfaces such as:

- hero content cards
- floating or semi-transparent navigation bars
- pricing cards
- demo or product-preview panels
- stat chips or testimonial highlight cards

### Where Not to Use Glassmorphism

Do not apply glass effects to content-heavy or utility-heavy UI where readability should dominate, including:

- long text sections
- FAQ answer bodies
- dense forms
- large content blocks
- every card on the page

### Practical Front-End Rules

- Maintain an approximate balance of **80% clean flat product UI**, **15% soft glass panels**, and **5% playful motion or illustration accents**.
- Keep blur, transparency, borders, and highlights subtle. Glass should feel refined, not loud.
- Preserve strong CTA visibility at all times. Important actions should never disappear into translucent surfaces.
- Use the Finch gradient palette sparingly and intentionally, anchored in **dark navy to red to orange**.
- Keep rounded sans-serif typography, strong contrast, and clear hierarchy as the primary visual anchors.
- In both light and dark mode, prioritize text legibility, accessible contrast, and clean separation between surfaces.
- If a glass treatment reduces comprehension, button clarity, or scan speed, remove it in favor of a flatter treatment.
